Recommendations

Drawing on the findings from both the heuristic evaluation and the usability test, as well as the background research on patient portal usability and user expectations, we have identified several opportunities to enhance the portal’s effectiveness, efficiency, and overall user satisfaction. Many of these align with established usability heuristics such as providing clear system feedback, preventing errors, supporting personalization, and offering accessible help. Others address gaps in service integration and navigation uncovered during testing. Implementing these recommendations would not only resolve the issues observed in our study, but also bring the portal more in line with best practices identified in the literature.

  1. System Feedback & Error Prevention
    • Add animation or visual indicators to show system status when information is processing.
    • Introduce a confirmation dialog box for critical actions (e.g., “Are you sure you want to download this file?”).
    • Warn users before they send a message to a provider they have not seen recently or at all, and suggest previously contacted doctors to reduce errors.
  2. Navigation & Task Efficiency
    • Integrate all appointment scheduling into a single, unified interface.
    • Provide in-portal, service-specific scheduling guidance (e.g., for vaccinations).
  3. Personalization & Customization
    • Allow users to personalize which information appears on their homepage/dashboard (e.g., Lab Results, Prescriptions).
    • Enable collapsing and expanding of dashboard sections for easier scanning.
  4. Help & Support
    • Offer more visible help options through an FAQ chatbot or integrated help widget.
    • Provide in-portal references that are more comprehensive, accurate, and clearly explained to better support lab result interpretation.
  5. Security
    • Review and, if necessary, enhance login authentication protocols to ensure user security without creating unnecessary barriers.
